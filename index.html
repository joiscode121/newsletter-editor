A Season of Devotion & Community
Our dedicated team at Texas Gaushala, Waller, Texas
Welcome to Texas Gaushala! Step into a sanctuary where modern care meets ancient tradition. Since 2020, we have been a dedicated home for indigenous cows and diverse farm life, including our resident peacocks and ducks. Today, our sanctuary is proud to nurture around 60 cows in a natural, healthy environment designed for their well-being and your peace of mind. We believe that true change is possible when hearts and hands work together. Driven by innovation and a deep-rooted passion for animal welfare, we invite you to join hands with us. Whether you are here to connect with our animals through therapeutic cow cuddling or to support our mission through cow adoption and sponsorship, your presence makes a difference. Thank you for being part of our journey toward a more compassionate and sustainable future.

S1

Community Spirit: Gaushala Pongal Celebration

On January 17, 30 volunteers gathered to honor the harvest season during Mattu Pongal, a day dedicated to honoring cattle for their role in agriculture.

Sacred Pooja: A morning ceremony offered thanks and blessings for the cattle. Prasad Preparation: Volunteers cooked traditional Pongal over an open fire, symbolizing prosperity and communal harmony.

S2

Warmth and Worship: Lohri Celebrations

The spirit of Lohri brought a glow to the Gaushala on January 17. From 4:00 PM to 6:30 PM, 30 volunteers celebrated around the sacred campfire, offering grains and sweets to the fire, symbolizing the casting away of the old and the welcoming of a prosperous new beginning.

S3

A Divine Encounter: Cows Visit Meenakshi Temple

Devotees at temple

An estimated 1,000 devotees joined at the Meenakshi Temple on January 17 between 10:00 AM and 1:00 PM.

Gau Pooja: Cows were honored with traditional rituals. Feeding the Sacred: Devotees offered fresh greens and treats, embodying the festival's message of gratitude.

S4

Harvest Blessings: Telugu Community Celebration

Our cows visited the local Telugu community on January 17, where 100 devotees gathered from 10:00 AM to 12:00 PM. Members offered prayers and traditional decorations, celebrating them as the "Gomata" (Mother Cow). Families and children participated in Gau Seva, offering fresh fodder and treats.

Sankranthi celebration
S5

A Day of Joy: Family Field Trip to the Gaushala

The Gaushala opened its gates for a special day of connection. Children delighted in interacting with the animals, learning about the importance of Gau Seva. Whether feeding cattle, walking through serene pastures, or enjoying a picnic under the open sky, the sense of community was undeniable.

February 2026
S6

Rudrabhisheka and Rudra Homa Event

Rudrabhisheka Pooja

On February 14, Texas Gaushala hosted a Rudrabhisheka and Rudra Homa led by the SVBF temple. About 10 Ritwiks chanted Veda mantras while nearly 100 people attended. Lunch was served at the conclusion.

This special event fell on Shani Trayodashi, one day prior to Maha Shivaratri, and also on Valentine's Day. It marked the 3rd Quarterly event at Texas Gaushala.

Rudra Homa: Ritwiks chanting Veda mantras at Texas Gaushala
S7

Special Guest: Madhavi Latha Visits the Gaushala

Texas Gaushala was honored to host Kompella Madhavi Latha, a prominent politician, entrepreneur, and philanthropist associated with the BJP in Telangana. She contested as the BJP candidate for the Hyderabad Lok Sabha constituency in 2024.

Her insightful talk placed the sacred cow at the heart of the conversation. Our sincere thanks to her and her team.

Calf Spotlight: Meet Narayan

Calf Narayan

Narayan was born to Radha in late 2025. He had some complications initially and was not readily accepted by his mother, but he continued to be strong. Today he is having fun with lots of other new calves born at the ranch.

He loves to be around visitors and can walk around fearlessly in between cows and humans. A true symbol of resilience and the loving care that Texas Gaushala provides.
